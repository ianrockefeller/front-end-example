Fire

Smoke Detectors

Smoke detectors identify smoke particles that could possibly be an indicator of the early stages of a fire. They are located through out the house, one per level minimum.

Heat Dectectors

Heat detectors should be installed in areas that smoke detectors do not perform well. These are usually areas that are exposed to higher or lower temperatures such as an attic or garage. High dust concentration may cause false alarms in smoke detectors.

Conventional Smoke Detectors

Standalone smoke detectors are battery powered and are not connected to any fire alarm system to contact the fire department for an emergency response. They are only capable of alerting people nearby that there is smoke and not saving your life.
